Farmer's guide

Rural APP

Developed for small rural producers, farmer's guide is an app that helps them identify the best time to spray agricultural pesticides

The challenge

The challenge was helping small farmers' producers to reduce time and resources when they needed to spray pesticides in their plantations and receive other important information about their region.

My role

In this project, I was responsible for:

  • Design Strategy
  • Product Design
  • UX/UI Design

The project

Brazilian farmers don't have money and resources, and they always try to not waste them.

The project was fully funded by Sebrae, which is a huge social institution that helps the development of micro and small enterprises.

The company offers the app totally free for any user.

Discovery phase

We started trying to understand the problem, so we started to make a basic field research.

So, we went to a region full of small farmers' properties and ask them what costs more, or what is more expensive in their job.

They said that the weather and the time to spray pesticides were the most painful and spendable. At this point, we discovered what is the features that we will apply.

So, what came to me was these results:

  • Farmers don't know when it's the best time to spray the pesticides;

  • Small farms must know what is the best time to spray;

  • A common issue for small farmers in Brazil, because weather changes and small farmers waste a lot of resources and money;

Competitive Analysis

So, based on these results, I started to search competitors, to understand if this question already has a solution.

To my surprise, I didn't find a bunch of competitors, and what I found was just English information (Brazilians speak Portuguese) those solutions seem to be adaptable to foreigners' info, and most parts of the information it's not easy to understand. Also, based on user feedback, we know that they don't use any kind of app or solution.

Primary hypothesis

So, I started to understand those points and started to create basic research, that helped me to see the main hypothesis: Is that really useful?

Primary research

I decided to move with simple paper research, with qualitative and open questions, and we had a chance to make this in a huge rural event in a state in Brazil.

The questions were:

  • How do you know when is the best time to spray pesticides? How important is this information?
  • Do you use any type of application or service? If yes, how does it help you?
  • How do you know about the occurrence of pests in your region? How important is this information?
  • How do you know when is the best time to spray pesticides? How important is this information?
  • Do you use some type of application or service? If yes, how does it help you?

Analyzing the data

Totally 30 farmers, and 120 qualitative answers, were collected. After that, we compile the answers and transform into numbers:

About the pesticides:
  • 90% don't use apps information;
  • 98% is important information;
  • 80% use personal experience;
About the pests:
  • 85% don't use apps information;
  • 89% is important information;
  • 85% use the personal;
